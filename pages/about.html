About Me

Summary

My research interests focus on stochastic modeling, particularly queueing control, with a growing emphasis on integrating learning-based methods with model-based control and decision-making to develop interpretable and theoretically grounded policies.

Education

  • Ph.D. Cornell University, Ithaca, New York, USA 2025
  • B.S. University of Science and Technology of China (USTC), Hefei, Anhui, China 2018

Research

Stochastic Modeling: Control of Queues (Aug 2020 – Aug 2025)
Advised by Professor Mark E. Lewis and Professor Jamol Pender

  • Simulation: Addressed national staffing problems of general physicians based on a network simulation model, understanding the impact of state regulations on staffing across states.
  • Optimal Control: Characterized structural properties of optimal policies for Markov Decision Processes (MDP) across all parameter and state spaces.
  • Heuristics: Designed simple and scalable heuristics for real-time decisions with demonstrated performance through theoretical analysis and numerical experiments.
  • Learning-based Policies: Extended MDP formulation and policies to high-dimensional queueing networks using reinforcement learning for approximate and explainable solutions.

Optimization under Uncertainty: Robust Control, Stochastic Programming, and Distributionally Robust Optimization (Jan 2019 – Aug 2020)
Process-Energy-Environmental Systems Engineering Lab

  • Data-driven Optimization: Constructed machine-learning-based uncertainty sets capturing data scope and ambiguity sets capturing distributional information.
  • Tractability: Reformulated models into tractable linear and mixed-integer programming formulations with efficient solution algorithms; extended to multi-stage frameworks via parameterized decision rules.
